Why a negotiation-first marketplace?

Fixed prices don’t always reflect reality. Your platform can let buyers and sellers discover a fair price together, without chaos.

Structured offers

Clean, rule-based bargaining

Buyers tap “Make an offer”, sellers respond with accept / reject / counter. Max rounds, expiry times, and minimum prices keep things under control.

Escrow-ready

Pay only when the deal is agreed

Turn accepted offers into orders instantly. Add secure payments later so the platform can hold funds until dispatch, building trust on both sides.

Transparent history

Every offer logged

Each step of the negotiation is saved – who offered what and when. This reduces disputes and gives you valuable pricing analytics in the future.

How it works for your users

Buyers, sellers, negotiation, payment – all in one straightforward flow you control.

1

Sellers list products

Each product has a list price and a minimum acceptable price. You control which categories support negotiation.

2

Buyers make offers

From the product page, buyers enter their offer and quantity. Low-ball offers below the minimum can be blocked automatically.

3

Back-and-forth

The seller counters or accepts. You can limit negotiation rounds (for example, 5) and add expiry timers to keep momentum.

4

Deal → Order → Payment

Once accepted, the final price becomes an order. Payment and shipping happen inside your platform to keep everything safe.

Questions you might get asked

Use these answers with investors, partners, or developers who want to understand the model.

Is this like Amazon?

The core difference is that prices aren’t always fixed. Products have a list price, but buyers can make offers and sellers can respond inside the platform, with clear rules and a full history of the negotiation.

How does the platform earn money?

You can charge commission per completed order, offer paid “priority listing” for sellers, or introduce subscription tiers with advanced analytics and tools for power sellers.

What about fraud and disputes?

Because every step of the negotiation is logged and payments can be held in escrow until dispatch, you have a strong foundation to resolve disputes and detect suspicious patterns.

Does this support mobile apps later?

Yes. This website is your concept home. A developer or no-code tool (like FlutterFlow or Bubble) can turn the same flows into a full mobile and web application later.
